Milkha Singh

Milkha Singh (20 November 1929 - 18 June 2021), also known as "The Flying Sikh", was an Indian track and field sprinter who was introduced to the sport while serving in the Indian Army.

He is the only athlete to win gold at 400 metres at the Asian Games as well as the Commonwealth Games.He also won gold medals in the 1958 and 1962 Asian Games.

From beginnings that saw him orphaned and displaced during the Partition of India, Singh has become a sporting icon in his country. In 2008, journalist Rohit Brijnath described Singh as "the finest athlete India has ever produced".

Singh died from complications of COVID-19 on 18 June 2021, at the age of 91, five days after his wife, Nirmal Saini.

Early Life and Partition

  • On October 20, 1929, Milkha Singh was born in Govindpura, a tiny village in Punjab, British India.
  • In 1947, when India was divided, there was a great deal of violence and eviction. During this time, Milkha Singh also lost his parents and siblings.
  • Milkha Singh, who saw the atrocities of the partition firsthand, relocated to Delhi and started working as a labourer before enlisting in the Indian Army.

Journey in Athletics

  • When Milkha Singh was a member of the Indian Army, he took part in inter-service athletic competitions, where his gift for running was found.
  • He first took part in the 200 and 400 metre races, but his speed and stamina allowed him to succeed in the 400 metre race.
  • Singh's success as an athlete was greatly influenced by his diligence, self-control, and resolve.

Melbourne Olympics 1956

  • His first international sporting occasion was the 1956 Melbourne Olympics, where Milkha Singh represented India.
  • Despite not making it to the 400-meter final race, the experience inspired him to put in more effort and strive for greater success in the future.

National Championships and Records

  • In 1958, Milkha Singh won gold in the 200 and 400 metre races at the Indian National Games.
  • In India, he broke numerous records in a variety of sporting competitions, including the 200, 400, and 4x400 relay.

Rome Olympics 1960

  • Milkha Singh was the first Indian athlete to advance to an Olympic athletics event final at the 1960 Rome Olympics.
  • With a time of 45.6 seconds, he came in fourth place in the 400 metre race and narrowly missed earning a medal.
  • Then-President of Pakistan, General Ayub Khan, gave Milkha Singh the moniker "The Flying Sikh" in honour of his extraordinary performance and speed.

Life After Athletics

  • Milkha Singh devoted his life to coaching and developing sports in India after quitting competitive athletics in 1964.
  • He established the Milkha Singh Sports Trust with the mission of helping gifted athletes from disadvantaged backgrounds succeed in their chosen sports.

Biographical Film "Bhaag Milkha Bhaag"

  • The life and tribulations of Milkha Singh were portrayed in the biographical sports movie "Bhaag Milkha Bhaag," which was released in 2013.
  • Farhan Akhtar played Milkha Singh in the critically acclaimed and financially successful movie, which helped to popularise his life's work and inspire many people.

Legacy and Inspiration

  • Generations of athletes in India and elsewhere continue to be inspired by Milkha Singh's transformation from refugee to internationally known athlete.
  • He has become a legendary character in Indian sports history as a result of his outstanding accomplishments and unwavering spirit, personifying perseverance, resilience, and the quest for perfection.
